I.Amplifying


Here's a little rundown of the technique known as Amplifying.

Hypertone begins at 1.200.000 beats per minute (20.000 Hz, the upper limit of human hearing). Despite this, most hypertone tends to be audible due to an effect called Aliasing. In short, if a sound contains a frequency that is too high for the DAW to process, that frequency is reflected back down to lower frequencies. Only frequencies below half the sampling rate can exist in a project file. One of the constant methods of making Hypertone is amplification, where you amplify a tone to make it audible again.
